Label iQ Demo Walkthrough

Drug Label AI Assistant - Powered by Denodo AI SDK

1
Login Screen

When you first visit Label iQ, you'll see the login dialog with role-based access control:

  • Patient - Basic drug information access
  • Physician - Full access to all drug data
  • Judge - Full access + SQL query visibility
2
Role Selection

Click the role dropdown to select your access level. Each role has different permissions for viewing drug information.

For this demo, we'll use the Physician role which provides full access to all 9 database views.
3
Enter Credentials

Enter your username and password to sign in. Credentials match the role name:

Role Username Password
Patient patient patient
Physician physician physician
Judge judge judge
4
Drug Selection Grid

After logging in, you'll see a grid of 13 drug logos. Click any drug to start asking questions about it.

Available drugs include: Biktarvy, ELIQUIS, ENBREL, ENTRESTO, FARXIGA, HUMIRA, IBRANCE, Imbruvica, JAKAFI, JANUVIA, JARDIANCE, Lantus, and Linzess.
5
Chat Interface

When you click a drug logo, the chat interface opens with a pre-filled question: "What is [Drug] used for?"

This format works reliably with the Denodo AI SDK. Simply click Send to submit.

6
AI Response with Metadata

The AI responds with detailed drug information pulled from the Denodo Agora database. The response includes:

  • Answer - Clear, formatted drug information
  • Data Sources - Which database views were queried
  • Response Time - How long the AI took to respond
Response time is typically 20-60 seconds. The first query may take longer due to cold start.
7
Follow-up Questions

Ask follow-up questions naturally. The AI maintains conversation context and remembers which drug you're discussing.

Example follow-ups:

  • "What are the side effects?"
  • "How should I take this medication?"
  • "Are there any drug interactions?"
8
Logout

Click the logout button in the header to end your session and return to the login screen.

Your conversation history is cleared when you log out.
